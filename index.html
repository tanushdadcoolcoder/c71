Campus Life

school church

was estabilished in 1950

Berachah Church was founded by C. W. Colgan, an oil company executive, who created it to evangelize Christianity. In 1950, Robert Thieme came to Berachah. Thieme met with the Board of Deacons at the close of his first Sunday service and recommended that they resign immediately. The board accepted this and from that moment on, according to Joe Wall, Thieme "became the dominant leader who brooked no challenge to his authority."[2] Thieme was pastor at Berachah until he retired in 2003, at which time his only son, Robert "Bobby" Thieme III, was elected by the congregation of Berachah to serve as the new pastor.[3]



school library

was build in 1980

The school has a well-equipped computerized library with a good selection of books. The library also provides students and teachers with a variety of reference books, encyclopedia, journals and newspapers, as well as internet access to supplement their classroom activities and information. Qualified, well-trained and enthusiastic library staff assist library users in checking out books and making recommendations for new books. For a smooth learning process, the library provides each student with access to necessary resources and learning materials.



school auditorium

was built in 1982

Our school's infrastructure is incomplete without the auditorium. It can accommodate over 750 people. The fully air-conditioned hall, which features a large stage, cutting-edge sound, lighting and audiovisual systems, as well as specially designed flooring and balcony spaces can host a range of activities and functions including indoor sports. It is equipped with an efficient projector.
